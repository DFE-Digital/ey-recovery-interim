Understanding child development and the EYFS

As an early years practitioner, understanding how children develop will enable you to meet the needs of all the children in your care. This module will help you to reflect on why knowledge of child development is important and help you to effectively implement the EYFS. It also considers the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on children.

What you'll learn

On completion of this module you will be able to:

  • establish and explore the stages of children’s brain development
  • explore the impact of brain development on children’s holistic development
  • examine the connection between brain development and children’s learning
  • explore a range of theories underpinning best practice

This module has been created by three early years experts.

Debbie Parry spent several years working as an early years practitioner in private day nurseries, before going on to use her knowledge and experience to teach the practitioners of the future.

Melissa Tharm worked in SEND residential environments for a number of years and now brings her experience and knowledge to training programmes to professionals in the health, care and education sectors.

Catherine Mcelroy has over 20 years' experience working in private day nurseries with children under 5. She has spent the last 10 years as a nursery manager, ensuring that children and their familities experience excellent care that is fun and exciting.
