That's not quite right

The Equality Act 2010 states that providers must make reasonable adjustments to include children who have a disability and allow fair access to the curriculum. It is important that children and people who have a disability are not directly or indirectly discriminated against because of their disability.

One of the main principles of the SEND provisions in the Children and Families Act 2014 is that parents, carers and children must be involved in all decisions relating to their support. This implies [among other things] that they will be provided with both:

  • ongoing, clear information about services
  • next steps to support their development and well-being

The Equality Act 2010 identifies 9 protected characteristics that are protected by law against any direct or indirect discrimination, including:

  • age
  • disability
  • gender reassignment
  • marriage and civil partnership
  • pregnancy and maternity
  • race
  • religion or belief
  • sex
  • sexual orientation

The EYFS statutory framework for group and school-based providers,(DfE 2024) para 3.61 states:

“Providers must have arrangements in place to support children with Special Education Needs and Disabilities (SEND). Maintained schools, maintained nursery schools and all providers who are funded by the local authority to deliver early education places must take into account the Special Educational Needs Code of Practice. Maintained schools and maintained nursery schools must identify a member of staff to act as Special Educational Needs Co-ordinator (SENCO) and other providers (in group provision) are expected to identify a SENCO. Providers may find it helpful to familiarise themselves with the early years section of the SEND Code of Practice.”

The EYFS statutory framework for childminders (DfE 2024) para 3.60 states:

“Childminders must have arrangements in place to support children with Special Educational Needs and Disabilities (SEND). Childminders are encouraged to identify a person to act as a SENCO (SEND coordinator). Childminders who are registered with a CMA, or who are part of a network, may wish to share the role between them. Childminders may find it helpful to familiarise themselves with the early years section of the SEND Code of Practice.”
