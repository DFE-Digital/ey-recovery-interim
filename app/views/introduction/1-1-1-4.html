The potential impact of limited child development knowledge

Limited knowledge of child development may lead to unintentional consequences for the children in your care.

Incorrect level of challenge

An incorrect level of challenge for children can be a contributing factor to incidents of unwanted behaviour.

When the environment and activities are not challenging enough, a child may complete tasks quickly or become bored. They may then start on an alternative task or entertain themselves with a destructive or distracting activity.

When the environment and activities are too challenging - a child may become frustrated and disengaged if they do not understand expectations or are afraid of failure.They may look to divert attention in other ways - which can often lead to unwanted behaviour.

Both situations lead to missed opportunities to learn, develop and thrive.

Delayed support

Incomplete knowledge of child development may prevent you from identifying where a child requires support in a particular area of development. This may delay them getting help early on.

Your knowledge of child development will help you to create and implement an effective curriculum. This reduces the gaps in learning and so removes the need for additional support.

Practitioner prompt

Do you have a child or children who are disengaged or disruptive?

What strategies have you tried so far? Have they made a difference?

Have you considered the child holistically? Have you looked at each area of development? Have you evaluated the environment?

Children can quickly become labelled as behaving in a certain way and this becomes their accepted behaviour. Behaviour always has a cause but have you been able to work out the true cause?

How could further knowledge regarding child development support this?

What do your colleagues think?
